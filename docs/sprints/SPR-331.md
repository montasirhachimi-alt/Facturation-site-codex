# SPR-331 — Executive Dashboard Revolution

## Executive Summary

SPR-331 transforms the Dashboard from a visually clean widget collection into an executive business cockpit. The sprint changes the story and hierarchy of the Dashboard only. It does not change Runtime, Services, business logic, data, routes, Prisma, database, APIs, permissions, backend behavior or AI logic.

## What Was Wrong Before

- The Dashboard contained useful sections, but every area had similar visual importance.
- Quick actions appeared too early, before the CEO could understand business health or priorities.
- KPIs existed as isolated metrics rather than a business heartbeat.
- Priorities and activity were present, but they did not clearly answer "what should I do first?"
- The page felt more like a report surface than a morning command center.

## What Changed

- Rewrote the hero story around the day: attention items, outstanding cash and pipeline.
- Moved quick actions below the executive read, making them secondary.
- Renamed and repositioned KPI cards as `Santé business`.
- Added a strongly distinguished `Centre de priorité` section with a clear first action.
- Added a `Performance` layer after priorities to explain progress, cash and pipeline.
- Reframed recent activity as `Changements récents` with a cleaner executive timeline.
- Kept all links, routes and existing static data intact.

## Dashboard Hierarchy Improvements

The Dashboard now follows:

1. Executive Hero
2. Business Health
3. Priority Center
4. Performance
5. Recent Business Changes
6. Quick Actions

This flow answers:

- Is my business healthy today?
- What deserves attention?
- Where is the opportunity?
- What should I do first?

## Desktop Improvements

- The first screen now leads with summary, health and decision-making instead of action shortcuts.
- The priority center has a dark executive panel and ranked action rows.
- Performance cards sit after priorities as analysis, not interruption.
- Quick actions are available but no longer compete with the primary executive story.

## Mobile Improvements

- Mobile keeps the same executive order instead of simply stacking old widget blocks.
- Priority actions remain readable and tappable.
- Quick actions become compact rows below the main cockpit story.
