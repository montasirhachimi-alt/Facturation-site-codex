# SPR-325 — CRM Navigation Clarity Continuation

## Summary

SPR-325 finishes the CRM navigation clarity pass after the SPR-324 product experience redesign. The sprint keeps the CRM contextual model intact while making the sidebar and contextual workspaces easier to understand for first-time users.

## Objective

Reduce navigation ambiguity around Contacts, Activités, Réunions, Tâches and Notes without creating standalone pages or changing business logic.

## Already Completed

- Removed visible `via contact` and `via société` badges from CRM sidebar entries.
- Kept contextual navigation metadata internally.
- Routed Contacts and Activités toward Sociétés because those workspaces are company-scoped.
- Routed Réunions, Tâches and Notes toward an existing contact workspace because those workflows are contact-scoped.
- Preserved French sidebar labels and existing permissions, services and routes.
- `npm run typecheck` passed.
- `npm run build` passed with the known existing PDF preview image warning.

## Continuation Work

- Reviewed the current CRM sidebar, CRM overview, company context and contact context after the sidebar simplification.
- Removed the remaining visible `via société` micro-badge from the pipeline creation CTA and replaced it with calmer action wording.
- Polished company and contact activity panels so they explain contextual activity as a timeline tied to the current society or contact.
- Localized visible activity filter options and badges in the reviewed contextual panels.
- Confirmed the sidebar remains visually aligned with the SPR-324 Dashboard and Sales navigation structure.

## Product UX Decisions

- Contextual CRM items should not look like incomplete standalone modules in the sidebar.
- Contacts are explained as living inside Sociétés, so users are guided to the parent company workspace.
- Activités are explained as timelines attached to a society or contact, not as a separate global list.
- Réunions, Tâches and Notes are explained as contact-scoped follow-up tools.
- Helper text favors calm, direct French wording over technical route explanations.
- The product keeps progressive disclosure: the sidebar stays simple, while the relevant workspace explains the next step.
