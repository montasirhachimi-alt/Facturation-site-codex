# SPR-327 — CRM Experience 2.0

## Executive Summary

SPR-327 upgrades the CRM from a functional workspace into a more premium SaaS experience. The sprint focuses only on presentation and product experience: hierarchy, spacing, cards, tables, empty states, detail panels, filters, timeline readability, sticky context and interaction polish.

No Runtime, Services, Prisma, database, APIs, permissions, backend features, AI, modules or workflows were changed.

## Screens Redesigned

- CRM home workspace.
- Companies list workspace.
- Company detail workspace.
- Company contacts workspace.
- Contact detail workspace.
- CRM customer table surface.
- Opportunity pipeline workspace.
- Company and contact opportunity panels.
- Company and contact activity timelines.
- Contact meetings, tasks and notes panels.
- Shared CRM-facing entity headers, cards, tables, toolbars, search bars, pagination and empty states.

## UX Improvements

- Added a high-impact CRM home hero so the dashboard feels immediately different on open.
- Strengthened CRM KPI cards with larger values, bolder icon tiles, top accents and stronger shadows.
- Upgraded quick action and recent company cards so they attract attention instead of blending into the page.
- Made main CRM table headers visually distinctive with a dark title band and stronger row hover feedback.
- Improved status badges with live dot indicators and richer spacing.
- Stronger executive CRM home copy and more premium quick action cards.
- Cleaner KPI cards with larger values, softer shadows and clearer hover states.
- More polished tables with stronger headers, calmer hover rows and improved empty states.
- More refined company/contact identity headers with better action hierarchy and French wording.
- Sticky company/contact tabs so the workspace remains easier to navigate while scanning long pages.
- Sticky inspectors for company, contact and pipeline contexts on desktop.
- Better timeline presentation with stronger visual anchors and less mechanical row styling.
- Opportunity cards now scan faster with clearer value, probability, close date and status grouping.
- Contextual panels now use consistent rounded cards, filter fields, empty states and hover feedback.
- Shared CRM surfaces now use larger radii, softer shadows and better whitespace for a more modern feel.

## Design Decisions

- The previous pass was too subtle because it relied mostly on softer radii, spacing and hover states; this continuation adds stronger first-screen contrast and object-card hierarchy.
- The CRM home hero is intentionally the most visually distinctive CRM surface because it defines perceived product quality within the first five seconds.
- Use polish inside the existing CRM architecture instead of redesigning modules or workflows.
- Keep blue for primary actions, focus and active states, while reducing visual noise elsewhere.
- Make identity surfaces feel premium first because company and contact detail pages are high-value demo screens.
- Keep tables dense enough for business use but visually calmer through spacing, rounded controls and hover feedback.
- Use sticky context only where it helps: tabs and inspectors on larger screens.
- Improve shared entity UI primitives because CRM screens already depend on them and this creates consistency without new architecture.
